PRIVACY POLICY

Last updated: April 26, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how TurnEm ("we", "our", or "us") collects, uses, and protects information when you use our salon turn-management software and related communications, including SMS text messaging.

1. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of information:

  • Client information — name, phone number, and service history entered by salon staff to manage the queue and appointments.
  • Staff information — manicurist name, role, schedule, and authentication credentials for staff using the app.
  • Appointment data — scheduled times, services, and notes associated with bookings.
  • Operational data — turn order, completed services, and similar information needed to run the salon's day.

2. How We Use Information

We use the information we collect only to operate the service:

  • To run the salon queue and assign clients to manicurists.
  • To schedule and confirm appointments.
  • To send transactional SMS notifications to clients (see §3).
  • To provide reporting and history within the salon's own account.
  • To secure the service and prevent abuse.

3. SMS Messaging

Mobile information will not be shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. All categories of information described in this Privacy Policy exclude text messaging originator opt-in data and consent; this information will not be shared with any third parties.

What we send

If a client provides their mobile number to the salon, the salon may send transactional SMS messages relating to that client's visit, including:

  • Appointment confirmations and reminders.
  • Notifications that the client's manicurist is ready or that the client is next in the queue.
  • Updates about queue position or wait time.

Frequency

Message frequency varies depending on appointments and queue activity.

Cost

Message and data rates may apply, depending on your wireless carrier plan.

Opt-out and help

You can opt out of SMS at any time by replying STOP to any message. You can request help by replying HELP. After opting out you will receive one final confirmation message and will not receive further texts.

Consent

Clients provide consent to receive SMS by giving their phone number to the salon and agreeing to be contacted about their appointment or queue status. Consent is not a condition of receiving services in person.

4. How We Share Information

We do not sell personal information. We share information only as needed to operate the service:

  • Service providers — including Supabase (database hosting), Twilio (SMS delivery), and similar infrastructure providers acting on our behalf.
  • Legal compliance — when required by law, subpoena, or to protect rights and safety.

As stated above, mobile phone numbers and SMS opt-in data are never shared with third parties for marketing or promotional purposes.

5. Data Retention

We retain client and operational data for as long as the salon's account is active. Salons may delete client records at any time. SMS message logs are retained only as long as needed for delivery, troubleshooting, and compliance.

6. Security

We use industry-standard safeguards to protect data, including encrypted transport (HTTPS), encrypted storage where applicable, role-based access controls, and authentication for both salon staff and admin access. No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Your Choices

  • SMS — reply STOP to opt out at any time.
  • Access and deletion — contact your salon (or us at the email below) to request access to or deletion of your information.

8. Children

TurnEm is intended for use by adult salon staff and adult clients. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. A minor's information may appear in a salon record only when provided by a parent or guardian for the purpose of a service appointment.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision. Continued use of the service after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
